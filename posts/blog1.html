Japanese Nabe - Sukiyaki(すき焼き)

Ziqi Wang

January 25, 2021

The first time I had Sukiyaki, I was studying in Kanazawa, Japan. I remember it was in winter, our seminar professor took us to a very popular sukiyaki resturant in local. I was so exctied and suprised by the amazing taste.
After that, I was wondering if I could do this at home,well it turned out not that difficult to make it on our own.

Ingredients:

  • beef slice 300g
  • Japanese long onion(may substitute leek) 2 stalks
  • Udon 200g
  • enoki mushrooms 1 pac
  • grilled tofu 1 block
  • shiitake mushrooms 8 pieces
  • Chinese cabbage) 200g
  • Kikkoman Soy Sauce 100 ml
  • Manjo Hon Mirin (mirin) 100ml
  • sugar 3 Tbsp
  • water 200 ml

Directions:

  1. Cut the beef into 2 to 3 portions. Diagonally cut the long onions into 1 cm (1/2 in.) widths. Parboil the udon and then cut into easy-to-eat lengths. Cut the grilled tofu into 8 equal pieces. Remove the stems from the shiitake mushrooms and score a crisscross pattern on each cap.Chop up the Napa cabbage; remove the hard center stem from the chrysanthemum leaves and cut into 5 cm (2 in.) lengths.
  2. Combine Soy Sauce, Mirin,sugar and water to prepare the sukiyaki stock.
  3. Using a portable gas or electric countertop stove, heat up a large pan or wok, melt the beef tallow and grease the entire pan.
  4. Reduce the heat and add the long onions. When fragrant, add the beef and quickly cook both sides.
  5. When browned, pour in sukiyaki stock and bring to a simmer.
  6. Turn the heat to low, and add the remaining ingredients as space permits. Eat the ingredients as they finish cooking, replenishing the skillet with more ingredients. When the broth is reduced, replace it with some of the simmering liquid.
